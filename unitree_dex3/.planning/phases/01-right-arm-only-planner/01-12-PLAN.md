# Plan 01-12 — Fix Stale `latest_joint_positions_` in Post-Trajectory Hold & Ramp

## Status: ✅ Build-verified — user-verified as part of 01-12b

## Problem

After Plan 01-11 (publish-gap closure + 250 Hz ramp), the arm **snapped back to standing immediately** after trajectory completion instead of staying at the end-point.

## Diagnosis

`trajectoryCallback` runs on a `SingleThreadedExecutor`. For the entire duration of the callback (trajectory loop + hold loop + ramp loop), `lowstateCallback` is blocked and never fires. Therefore `latest_joint_positions_` remains frozen at the value from callback entry — which is the **standing pose**.

Consequences:
- **Hold loop** (`q = latest_joint_positions_[idx]`) was commanding the arm back to standing for 1 s @ 250 Hz.
- **`ramp_start_positions = latest_joint_positions_`** made the ramp start from standing, not the trajectory endpoint — the ramp had nothing to interpolate.

## Decision

Instead of relying on `latest_joint_positions_` (which is stale), **extract the trajectory endpoint directly from `msg->points.back()`** right after the trajectory loop.

## Edits

### `joint_trajectory_executor.cpp`

1. **New `trajectory_endpoint` computation** (after trajectory loop, before hold loop):
   - Initialize from `standing_pose` as baseline for non-trajectory joints.
   - Overwrite with each trajectory joint's `last_point.positions[j]` (clamped to URDF limits via `joint_limits_`).

2. **Hold loop**: change `q` source from `latest_joint_positions_[idx]` to `trajectory_endpoint[idx]`.

3. **Ramp start**: change `ramp_start_positions` assignment from `latest_joint_positions_` to `trajectory_endpoint`.

## Risk

None. This is a surgical data-source change; the hold loop and ramp logic are otherwise unchanged from 01-11.

## Follow-up

User reported after this fix: arm no longer snaps back, but **overshoots past the trajectory endpoint before slowly returning to standing**. Root cause identified as master discontinuity (0.5→1.0 jump between hold and ramp). Fixed in **01-12b**.

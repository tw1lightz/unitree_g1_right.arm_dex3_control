# Plan 01-08: Fade kp/kd through the end-of-trajectory ramp

## Why

User report after Plan 01-07 PASS:
> "现在的问题是虽然结束后不会抖动了,但会瞬间回到初始位置,速度太快了."

(Trajectory completion no longer jerks ✓. But after the 1 s hold ramp, the arm snaps back to standing too fast.)

## Root cause

Plan 01-07 made each ramp frame publish `q = latest_joint_positions_, kp = 60, kd = 1.5` (same stiffness as the trajectory-following frames). That correctly eliminated the q=0 jerk at frame 0. But this stiffness now **persists for the entire 1 s ramp**: even when `kNotUsedJoint.q` has faded to e.g. 0.1, the planner's `kp=60` is essentially saying "stay exactly here", which dominates whatever blend the body controller is trying to apply (its standing-pose target is far from the trajectory end-point). When `kNotUsedJoint.q` finally reaches 0.0, the planner's authority drops to zero in one tick, body has 100% authority, and it servos toward standing with whatever its own internal kp is — fast.

Net effect: the user sees "hold for 1 s, then snap to standing". Plan 01-07 traded a q=0 jerk for a body-controller snap.

## Constraint analysis

We need the body controller to gain effective influence **before** the master switch fully releases. Two natural ways:

1. **Make the planner's hold weaker over time.** Linear-fade `kp` from 60 → 0 and `kd` from 1.5 → 0 over the ramp duration. By midway, the planner is barely fighting the body controller; by the end, planner has no influence even if `kNotUsedJoint.q` were still > 0. This lets the body controller progressively pull the arm toward standing during the ramp; by the time the master switch reaches 0.0, the arm is already most of the way home.
2. **Tell the planner to move toward standing.** Linearly interpolate `q` from `latest_joint_positions_` toward a known standing pose. Requires knowing the standing pose for every joint — `g1_dex3_joint_defs.hpp` has no such constants, and a launch parameter would push the configuration burden onto the operator. Out of scope.

Approach 1 wins on simplicity and robustness: we don't need to know the body controller's standing target at all; we just stop fighting it.

Extending the ramp duration from 1 s to 3 s also helps perceptually — the user previously selected 3 s for Plan 01-04's `gracefulRelease` and seemed satisfied with that pacing. The total over-ramp time being 3 × longer just means the kp-fade midpoint (most of the actual movement) lands in the 1.5 s neighborhood, which feels natural for a "let the arm settle" motion.

We keep `q = latest_joint_positions_` (Plan 01-07's fix) so frame 0 still doesn't jerk: at t=0, `kp = 60, q = current_actual` continues to hold the arm exactly where the trajectory ended, just like Plan 01-07. From there, kp linearly decays.

## Decision

Single surgical edit in `trajectoryCallback`'s end-of-trajectory ramp (`@/home/unitree/Desktop/unitree_dex3/src/unitree_g1_dex3_stack-main/src/joint_trajectory_executor.cpp:235-260`):

- Change `interp_duration` from `1.0` to `3.0`.
- Change `interp_steps` from `50` to `150` (preserves 20 ms cadence).
- Replace the constant `kp = 60.0f, kd = 1.5f` lines with `kp = (1.0 - t) * 60.0f, kd = (1.0 - t) * 1.5f`.

Three numeric edits inside the existing for-loop, no structural change.

## Notes

This is the third compound-bug-revelation Plan in the executor (01-04 → 01-06 → 01-07 → 01-08). The pattern of "fix one bug, surface the next, fix it" continues to hold — each Plan addresses exactly one observable defect, and the reverification cycle on the live robot is what surfaces the next layer.

The only practical risk of approach 1: with `kp` faded to 0 and the body controller still ramping in, gravity could pull the arm down faster than the standing pose target servo can compensate, especially if the trajectory ended with the arm raised. If that becomes visible, the next iteration would either (a) bias the kp fade curve to stay higher longer (e.g. `kp = (1.0 - t)^2 * 60`), or (b) introduce the standing-pose launch parameter for active interpolation. We try the simple linear fade first.

# Plan 01-11: close publish gap + raise ramp publish frequency to 250 Hz

## Why

User confirmed two facts that constrain the diagnosis:
1. With our executor running, arm moves to goal — so our LowCmd is reaching firmware (asymmetric IDL: ROS 2 → bare DDS works; bare DDS → ROS 2 tools doesn't, which is why `ros2 topic hz` was silent).
2. Reference (`right_arm_mode.py`) produces a smooth slow return to standing on the same hardware. So the hardware/firmware path is fine; our executor logic has the bug.

Reading `joint_trajectory_executor.cpp` revealed two issues neither prior plan had addressed:

```@/home/unitree/Desktop/unitree_dex3/src/unitree_g1_dex3_stack-main/src/joint_trajectory_executor.cpp:234-244
    rclcpp::sleep_for(1s);  // Wait for the last command to take effect

    // After executing the trajectory, close the hand
    hand_cmd.data = true;
    hand_cmd_pub->publish(hand_cmd);
    // Wait for the hand to close
    rclcpp::sleep_for(1s);  // Wait for the hand to close
    RCLCPP_INFO(this->get_logger(), "Trajectory execution complete, returning to default pose.");
```

**Issue A — 2-second publish gap.** Between the last trajectory-waypoint LowCmd and the first ramp LowCmd, executor goes silent for 2 s. During that gap firmware sees no LowCmd from us, falls back to its internal default behavior, and starts dragging the arm toward standing. By the time the ramp begins, the arm has already drifted significantly. The "snap" the user perceives is the firmware's standing-servo activity during this gap, not anything in our ramp.

**Issue B — ramp frequency 50 Hz vs reference 250 Hz.** The ramp is `150 steps over 3.0 seconds` = 50 Hz. Reference's `smooth_exit` is `500 steps over 2.0 seconds` = 250 Hz. At 50 Hz, frames are 20 ms apart; firmware's internal control loops can run multiple times between our publishes and partly reclaim authority every frame.

## Decision

Two coordinated edits:

### Edit (a) — replace the 2 s sleep gap with a 1 s hold-publish loop @ 250 Hz

Build `hold_cmd` once with the same fields as the last trajectory frame:
- `motor_cmd[kNotUsedJoint].q = 0.5` (full planner authority during hold, same as trajectory frames)
- `motor_cmd[idx].mode = 1, kp = 60, kd = 1.5, dq = 0, tau = 0` for every right-arm joint
- `motor_cmd[idx].q = latest_joint_positions_[idx]` (snapshot taken just before the loop, ≈ trajectory end-point)

Publish it 250 times at 4 ms intervals (1 s total). On the first iteration also publish hand-close. Hand close happens in parallel (separate publisher) while arm is held stiff at the trajectory end-point.

Net change: the two `sleep_for(1s)` calls are replaced by one 1 s loop that does NOT leave a publish gap. Drops the total wait time from 2 s to 1 s (the original 2-s cushion was conservative; 1 s is enough for hand close).

### Edit (b) — bump ramp `interp_steps` from 150 to 750

Single-line change. `interp_duration` stays 3.0 s. Ramp now runs 750 steps over 3.0 s = 250 Hz with 4 ms sleeps. q-interpolation logic from Plan 01-09 unchanged. mode=1 from Plan 01-10 unchanged. kp/kd unchanged at 60/1.5.

Total continuous-publish duration after trajectory: 1 s hold + 3 s ramp = 4 s, at 250 Hz, no gap.

## Out of scope

- Reference numeric parity (kp 80, kd 3.0, kp_wrist 40, gravity FF). Defer to Plan 01-12 only if Plan 01-11 alone insufficient.
- The 2 s `sleep_for` originally suggested arm settle time + hand close time. Settling at the trajectory end-point happens for free during the new hold-publish (PD control IS settling). Hand close timing is the only legitimate justification, and 1 s is enough.

## Notes

This plan is the architectural fix Plans 01-04..01-10 were missing. Each prior plan tweaked what we publish; none noticed we were silent for 2 s right when smoothness mattered most. Lesson: **when iterative tuning of payload doesn't fix a smoothness bug, audit the publish timeline for gaps.** Reference implementations are tight 250 Hz publish loops with no breaks — that itself is part of the contract.
